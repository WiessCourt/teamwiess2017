Welcome to Wiess College!

We here at Wiess are all so excited to meet you, but without further adue, here's some information about the college where you will spend an amazing four years. First, we have a number of useful and unique spaces available to students. The space below are open to students at any time, however if you want to ensure a room's availability during a certain time, you can reserve a room by contacting your Wiess secretary Meagan Dwyer.

PDR

The private dining room is a quiet room attached to the Commons is a nice place to have meetings and is frequented by the A-Team and the Rice Philharmonics. There’s a lot of Wiess history in here, including pictures of all our former Masters, and a lot of old Campaniles as well. Look for pictures of Dr. Byrd as a student, or camp out to get down to business.

Third Floor Kitchen

If you want to bake something, make popcorn, or even cook dinner on those Saturday nights when the server is closed, the Third Floor Kitchen is the place to do it. We just completely redesigned it with new counters, new tile, and new stove! Also, ask the Kitchen Reps (Ian Mellor-Crummey and Molly Cisneros) for access to baking supplies.

Dance Theater

Also unique to Wiess is our dance theater, complete with speakers, nice wood floors, and full-length mirrors. The dance theater is perfect for choreographing your latest Youtube hit or your dance for the O-Week Talent Show. IT is located on the second floor below the RA suites.

Commons

Wiess is home to the largest commons on campus, which means lots of space but maybe not the best lighting at night. During the day, enjoy our huge windows while eatin lunch with fellow Wiessmen, or find a quiet table to study on late at night. It also has a piano which is frequented by our musicians. Most big events at Wiess such as NOD, Salsa Night, Tabletop performances, and even our Superbowl watching party, are held in the Commons.

Movie Room

The movie room: stuff of legend and the desire of every other college on campus. WE have brand new, extremely comfortable couches, stadium seating, and an even newer projector to replace our old TV. Old Wiessmen tell of the days of surround sound, but worse couches. This is a great place to spend a Friday night with some friends and lots of popcorn. Don’t forget to check out the huge Wiess movie collection, housed by our Movie Reps. The Wiess Movie Room is located on the fourth floor by the elevator.

Sparky's

Sparky’s used to be a Wiess storage closet, but has been slowly transformed in the past five years. This goldenrod room is a great place to study during the day, jam to some karaoke or play poker at night, or even watch some powderpuff. The air hockey and ping pong tables have also been relocated to Sparky’s. Sparky’s is located on the fourth floor right next to the movie room and tucked away in the corner.

Wiess Housing

At Wiess we are also lucky to have some of the nicest (in the author's humble opinion) rooms on the entire campus! Take a look at the luxurious and spacious accommodations available to you for your first year.

Your Suite

For your first year, you will be assigned to what we call a double—a suite with four people in two rooms and a shared common room. The bedrooms and common rooms are each approximately 16' x 12'. Each suite also has its own bathroom, which means no trekking down the hall in the middle of the night when you need to use the bathroom. Be advised that the bathrooms have no cabinetry so buying a plastic storage container is advisable, especially for those of you who have lots of toiletry items.

Your Common Room

In the common room you will find a bookcase (pictured below), tiled flooring, and space to be adventurous. Wiessmen often choose to outfit their common rooms with couches, recliners, mini-fridges, etc., but if you are apprehensive about transporting large furniture, rest assured that there almost always tend to be cheap furniture floating around with “a lot of sentimental value.” If that doesn't work out there are also great (read: economical) furniture places in Houston, and you can always try your luck with Craigslist. Most common rooms can very comfortably fit a large couch or two small couches, your mini-fridge and a TV stand (to give you a better idea about size).

If you're planning to share appliances or entertainment devices (e.g. TV, DVD player) with your roommate and suitemates, make sure to communicate with them in advance. You probably don't want to end up with three mini-fridges and no microwave (or do you?).

Your Bedroom

The standard bedroom furniture for each student is a three-drawer dresser, a wardrobe closet (pictured to the right), a desk, a desk chair and a twin EXTRA LONG (make sure you get the right size sheets!) bed that is bunkable and can be lofted to different heights. Most people don't find it necessary to bunk their beds, and there's not much of a need for more furniture in you bedroom, unless you want to have a special desk chair or a simple bookshelf.

Dr. Alexander Byrd

Associate Professor - History

Email: axb@rice.edu

Phone #: 713-348-2541

The Byrds—there are four of them, two larger and two smaller—are anxious to meet you! Dr. Byrd teaches in the department of History and has worked at the university since 1998. He was born in Hawaii, and grew up in Colorado Springs, Colorado as well as right here in Houston. A student of eighteenth-century Afro America and of modern African American life in the United States, he is especially interested in the history of the transatlantic slave trade and the course of school desegregation in the south. He’s just as keen, though, to hear about your plans for the world and what you want from your time at Rice and Wiess.

Mrs. Byrd is an educator and is currently the principal of Cummings Elementary School in West Houston. She hails from western Kentucky, and was educated at Berea College and at the University of London. If you are considering a career working with young people, she’s an excellent source of advice and information. Mrs. Byrd also loves to move! She’s addicted to early morning Boot Camp at the nearby YMCA, and is always down for a jog, a brisk walk, or impromptu aerobics. Like to start your Saturdays with a run? You don’t have to do it alone.

The Big Byrds have been married forever, and have picked up two other Byrds along the way: Benjamin and Jenna. Benjamin loves basketball, but perhaps loves classical and jazz guitar even more. Jenna’s tastes gravitate toward gymnastics and sprinting . The smallish Byrds are voracious readers and avid swimmers. Both have been educated in several of the city’s Spanish immersion schools. So code-switch with them (please).

The Byrds live adjacent to the college at the Wilson House (named for a long-time Wiess college RA). You’ll see them at meals, Wiess events, and across campus. They’re here to support the college (which is you!). So if you are ever feeling a little lost, don’t hesitate to mosey on over. They’re eager to help you navigate the university. And when you have a particularly proud moment, let them know. They want to celebrate with you. There will be ample opportunity to hang out with them at “the house.” Get ready for Solos and Duets study break, Twenty Minute Jazz Festival, Brunch Book Club, and other reasons to take a break from your daily grind with the family next door. In any case, they can’t wait to see all of the wonderful things you’ll do here over the next few years!
