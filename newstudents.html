Welcome to Wiess College!

We here at Wiess are all so excited to meet you, and you will be receiving a humongous book of information about Wiess shortly. If you're feeling impatient, here's some information about the college where you will spend an amazing four years. First, we have a number of useful and unique spaces available to students. The space below are open to students at any time, however if you want to ensure a room's availability during a certain time, you can reserve a room with your Wiess secretary Kaitlyn Swinney using this form.

Commons

Wiess is home to the largest commons on campus, which means lots of space and shockingly effective sound muffling. During the day, enjoy our huge windows while eating lunch with fellow Wiessmen, or find a quiet table to study on late at night. It also has a piano which is frequented by our resident musicians. Most big events at Wiess, such as NOD, Salsa Night, and even our Superbowl watching party, are held in the Commons.

Movie Room

The movie room is seriously the stuff of legend and the desire of every other college on campus. We have brand new, extremely comfortable couches, stadium seating, and a recently installed projector with 4k picture capabilities (people who understand technology claim this is very impressive). This is a great place to spend a Friday night with some friends and lots of popcorn, but be sure to check the Wiess calendar online to see if it's already been reserved! Also check out the huge Wiess movie collection, housed by our Movie Reps. It's located on the fourth floor by the elevator.

Laundry Room

When you inevitably find yourself running out of clothes and clean socks, fear not. Wiess has its very own laundry room, and the machines are free! Just remember to follow the rules: if someone's wet clothes are in the washing machine you need, move them to the dryer on low or medium heat, and if clothes in a dryer are still wet, run them again! Don't forget to bring High Efficiency detergent: good for your clothes and the environment. The laundry room is located on the first floor right by the Commons.

PDR

This quiet room attached to the Commons is a nice place to have meetings and is frequented by the A-Team and the Rice Philharmonics. There's a lot of Wiess history in here, including pictures of all our former Masters, and a lot of old Campanile's as well. Look for pictures of Dr. Byrd as a student, or camp out to get down to business.

Sparky's

Sparky's used to be a Wiess storage closet, but has been slowly transformed in the past five years in various different directions. This vibrant goldenrod room is a great place to study during the day, play various games with your friends, or even watch some powderpuff. We have lots of tables, mini basketball hoops, big windows, and of course, the Wiess crest. Sparky’s is located on the fourth floor right next to the movie room and tucked away in the corner.

Third Floor Kitchen

If you want to bake something, make popcorn, or even cook dinner on those Saturday nights when the servery is closed, the Third Floor Kitchen is the best place to do it. We just recently completely redesigned it, with new counters, new tile and new stoves! Ask the Kitchen Reps for access to any baking supplies you need.

Dance Room

Also unique to Wiess is our dance room, complete with speakers, nice wood floors and full-length mirrors. Perfect for choreographing your latest YouTube hit or your dance for the O-Week Talent Show. The dance room is located on the second floor below the RA suites.

Music Room

Don't wanna work? Wanna bang on the drum all day instead? Then check out our Music Room, located on the second floor right above the laundry room. It features a recording booth and soundproofing material that sort of works. It’s sure to rock anyone's world.

Wiess Housing

At Wiess we are lucky to have some of the nicest (in this webmaster's humble opinion) rooms on the entire campus! Take a look at the luxurious and spacious accommodations available to you for your first year below, and click here for a personal tour of your new room with 2015 OWeek Coordinator, Ryan Deal!

Your Suite

For your first year, you will be assigned to what we call a double—a suite with four people in two rooms and a shared common room. The bedrooms and common rooms are each approximately 16' x 12'. Each suite also has its own bathroom, which means no trekking down the hall in the middle of the night when you need to use the bathroom. Be advised that the bathrooms have no cabinetry so buying a plastic storage container is advisable, especially for those of you who have lots of toiletry items.

Your Common Room

In the common room you will find a bookcase (pictured below), tiled flooring, and space to be adventurous. Wiessmen often choose to outfit their common rooms with couches, recliners, mini-fridges, etc., but if you are apprehensive about transporting large furniture, rest assured that there almost always tend to be cheap furniture floating around with “a lot of sentimental value.” If that doesn't work out there are also great (read: economical) furniture places in Houston, and you can always try your luck with Craigslist. Most common rooms can very comfortably fit a large couch or two small couches, your mini-fridge and a TV stand (to give you a better idea about size).

If you're planning to share appliances or entertainment devices (e.g. TV, DVD player) with your roommate and suitemates, make sure to communicate with them in advance. You probably don't want to end up with three mini-fridges and no microwave (or do you?).

Your Bedroom

The standard bedroom furniture for each student is a three-drawer dresser, a wardrobe closet (pictured to the right), a desk, a desk chair and a twin EXTRA LONG (make sure you get the right size sheets!) bed that is bunkable and can be lofted to different heights. Most people don't find it necessary to bunk their beds, and there's not much of a need for more furniture in you bedroom, unless you want to have a special desk chair or a simple bookshelf.

Getting Involved at Wiess

From the start, there a numerous different ways to become involved in culture, life and leadership at Wiess College; below is a sampling of the many ways to get involved.

First Year Reps

The two First Year Reps will serve as voting members on Cabinet and work with the college government through responsibilities such as creating Wiess merchandise, organizing the Annual Christmas Tree Decorating Study Break, and planning other fun events for the college. Overall you will act as a voice for Wiess's new class!

SA New Student Reps

Each residential college has four NSRs who each serve within one of the Student Association's standing committees or a Student Initiative project. You will represent Wiess and work on projects pertaining to Rice!

Freshmen Council

The Freshman Council will serve as a group of freshmen who the Sophomore Reps will oversee. You will represent the class, plan projects and events for Wiess, and work together to implement new ideas for the college!

Wiess Associates Committee

As a member of the Associates Committee, you will work with Associates who are a part of Wiess. You will organize events, act as a liaison from Wiess, and make valuable connections with faculty, alumni, and community members and

Wiess Outreach Committee

On the Outreach Committee, you will get the opportunity to engage Wiess with service in the Houston community. You will work with other Wiessmen to volunteer at Outreach Days at various service sites well as plan events such as study breaks and events.

Shadowing Wiess positions

As a New student, you have the opportunity to "shadow" any Cabinet/Rep/Committee Position that interests you. As a shadow, you will learn the role and responsibility of the position and be able to get involved at the college in whichever area of Wiess interests you!

Being a Wiessman!

Whether or not you want a position at Wiess, there are ALWAYS opportunities for you to get involved in some way. You are now a member of Wiess and that means you have the chance to benefit and contribute in whatever way interests you. Yeah Wiess!
